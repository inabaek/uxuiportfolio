Conversation card game app

March/2021 - Ongoing | Personal project

"Good conversation makes the moment special"

My friend and I went on a trip. I brought a conversation card game called Table-tok. There are 140 question cards in a box. The questions made us talk about a wide variety of things and I got to know my friend better through the conversations. After playing the game my friend and I thought what if we make the conversation card game as a digital product? So that we do not have to carry the cards around and play the game wherever we go! After the trip, I researched conversation games in general, and the research led me to think about how we might make the game more engaging and attractive. What makes an enjoyable user experience?

Overview

Option 1

[Spending time on social media]

Option 2

[Spending time with your loved one]

Imagine you and your loved one are sitting on a sofa at home. Now, you have two options to spend time. One, spending time on social media such as Instagram, YouTube and Tic-Tok and etc. Two, having a meaningful conversation and make special memory between you and your loved ones. What would you choose to do? Let's say you decided to make a conversation with your loved one. But the thing is you don't know where to start. Therefore, we decided to make a 'Get To Know You' question card game app. So that you can make a meaningful and special memory with your loved ones.

User Research

Findings from user research

• Conversation with others keeps us healthier.
• It affects people's mental and physical health directly.
• People tend to socialise and enjoy deep conversation with others.
• Many people feel connected if they have a deep conversation.
• More then half of respondents responded they would try a conversation game app.
• Many of them like to play with in couple or friend/friends.

Hypotheses

• If we provide a variety of topics and different levels user can choose from, user can play different cards each play.

• If we add some gaming elements to attract the users, the users will visit the app more often.

• If we use qualitative data we will be able to provide the best-fit questions to user's situation.

Competitor Research

Findings from competitor research

• Most of digital card games are about fun quiz and humerus questions for parties
• Simple design and easy to use
• Too many pop-up ads
• Gaming elements such as buying coins
• Very limited cards to play
• Offering customised cards

Idea Sketch

I tried to put together the idea that I came up with.

* Click the images to view full-size in different tap

Onboarding: I wanted the user experiences onboarding pages to feel welcoming and exciting. Also, acquainting the user with a product.

Home: Because Home is where everything starts, I wanted to make it as simple and intuitive as possible. Each topic has a drop-down menu of levels to choose from. I came up with the idea of multiple-choice questions with a selection button on the top right, so that the user can have more options to play the game.

Create my questions: Through competitor research, regardless of the online or offline board game, I found out that many question card game products provide black cards to make customised questions for the users. I thought this idea would rise users' interest in using the product. Therefore, I wanted to make the "my question" section at home.

Save button: Since I decided to make the "My question" section, I thought it would also appropriate to make a save button on question cards. So that the users can save any questions they like throughout playing a game if they want to.

Shop / Settings: This app is categorized as a game. Therefore, I wanted to create gaming elements in the product. I came up with the idea of a shop where the users can buy some tokens to open a new level of question cards. This also can cover business needs as well as a design goal.

Mid-fidelity Prototype

After creating a low-fidelity wireframe, I made mid-fidelity prototyping to test the app in an early stage. So that I can have other people's opinions and get to fix some parts.

1st User testing and feedback

Response : 2

• [UI] On the Home page, I didn't recognise there was a drop-down menu until I click the button.

• [UI] Each question card only has text. It would be nice to have an appropriate illustration with the text.

Visual design colour palette

Colour Psychology: The Emotional Effects of Colour I wanted the design to be intuitive. I looked up some information about colour psychology and its impact on people's minds. I believe colour has a power to influence a person's emotions and state of mind. Therefore, I chose the common phycological effects of colours and used a different colour on each topic.
